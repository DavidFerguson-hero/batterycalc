How We Calculate Your Recommendation

Your Data

You upload one or more CSV files containing half-hourly electricity consumption readings (48 rows per day). Your data is sent securely to our server for analysis and is not stored beyond your session.

Tariff Modelling

Each tariff is defined by a rate for every half-hour slot across the day. Time-of-Use tariffs have a cheap overnight window and a more expensive daytime or peak rate. Standing charges are included in all annual cost figures.

Battery Simulation

For each day, the server runs a half-hourly simulation: the battery charges during cheap slots (subject to your charge rate and capacity) and discharges during expensive slots to offset consumption. Round-trip efficiency (default 90%) is applied on the charging side.

Payback & ROI

Payback is calculated by compounding the annual saving at your chosen inflation rate until cumulative savings equal the installed cost. Ten-year ROI = (annual saving × 10) − installed cost.

Limitations

  • The simulation resets battery state each day — no carry-over between days.
  • Battery degradation (~2%/yr) is not modelled.
  • Seasonal variation: your uploaded period is assumed representative of the full year.
  • Solar modelling uses PVGIS 2023 weather data; actual output varies with shading and soiling.
  • Tariff rates are indicative Q1 2026 published rates.

EDF FreePhase Dynamic — How It Works

What Makes It Dynamic?

Unlike FreePhase Static, FreePhase Dynamic sets new unit rates every day based on day-ahead wholesale electricity prices. The three-band structure (Green/Amber/Red) stays fixed; only the pence-per-kWh values change.

How This Calculator Models It

Because rates vary daily, this calculator uses representative recent averages: Green ~3.9p, Amber ~26.2p, Red ~43.1p. At 3.9p overnight the battery earns a healthy margin discharging into every non-green hour.

Why Results Could Differ

On low-price days the Green rate can approach zero (making charging nearly free). On high-demand days, Red can spike to the 75p cap. A smart battery that adjusts to day-ahead prices could capture significantly more value.
